I apologize if this was already discussed...
 
N tennis players enter a knock-out competition (the looser leaves the competition). All the players are of exactly the same playing strength, and the drawing was completely random.
 
What is the probability that 2 choosen players meet at  the competition?

on Dec 26th, 2007, 3:11pm, Hippo wrote:

 
 but I suppose there may be easier reasoning.

 
Yes there is:

 
Let Xk be the random variable which denotes the number of games played by the kth guy.
 
We have Sumk=1 to N Xk = 2(N-1)
 
Also Expectation for each Xk is the same: i.e. E[Xi] = E[Xj] for all i,j in [n]
 
Thus E[Xk] = 2(N-1)/N
 
If pij is the probability that person i and person j meet (which is same for any i =/= j pair, thus can be denoted by a constant p), then  
 
E[Xk] = p*(N-1) = 2(N-1)/N
 
Thus p = 2/N.

My reasoning was even simpler:
 
hidden:
By symmetry, each potential pairing is equally likely to occur in each slot in the competition. There are N-1 pairings during the course of the contest (each match eliminates one player) with no repeats (each match eliminates one player) out of N(N-1)/2 potential pairings, so there is a:
 
(N-1) / [N(N-1)/2] = 2/N
 
probability of any given pairing occurring.
